ACT I — IN WHICH THE CAPTAIN DEMANDS PHYSICS
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

[The stage: a Python terminal. Session 1 has ended. render_skippy.py exists.
 Skippy rotates in a GIF, silver-bodied, navy-coated, magnificent.
 But the Captain is not satisfied. He knows what Skippy is MADE of.]

CAPTAIN:
  ♩ Skippy's body — what is it, truly? ♩
  ♩ Not a color chosen by committee.   ♩
  ♩ Aluminum. A cylinder. A beer can.  ♩
  ♩ His coat is wool. Dyed navy blue.  ♩
  ♩ I want this to be a matter engine. ♩
  ♩ Rendering — a side-effect only.    ♩

SKIPPY:
  ♩ Then we must build the physics, Captain,    ♩
  ♩ from the electron up, no shortcuts taken.  ♩
  ♩ Drude's free electrons, oscillating free,  ♩
  ♩ plasma frequency from n_e.                 ♩
  ♩ Fresnel at the surface, Maxwell's law,     ♩
  ♩ reflectance from the complex n+ik raw.     ♩

[Skippy writes optics.py. The Drude model assembles itself.
 n_e computed from crystal density and valence count.
 ωp = √(n_e e²/mε₀). γ from measured resistivity.
 The cascade runs. The stage glows silver.]

SKIPPY:
  ♩ Aluminum is silver-white because         ♩
  ♩ its plasma frequency exceeds the cause   ♩
  ♩ of all visible light — each wavelength   ♩
  ♩ reflects with equal strength.            ♩
  ♩ Three electrons free per atom, Captain.  ♩
  ♩ The atom chose this. We did not.         ♩

CAPTAIN: [quietly, looking at the reflectance values]
  ♩ You didn't set it.                       ♩
  ♩ The information cascade set it.          ♩

ACT II — THE TRIAL OF THE COPPER ATOM
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

[A discordant chord. The Copper Atom enters, dressed in pink.
 This is wrong. Copper is warm orange. Something has gone terribly awry.]

SKIPPY: [alarmed]
  ♩ Copper emerges pink — (0.81, 0.58, 0.74)! ♩
  ♩ The Lorentz oscillator has gone too far.   ♩
  ♩ The interband parameters, Rakić's own,     ♩
  ♩ were entered wrong — strength 0.562,        ♩
  ♩ when correct values hover near 0.003.      ♩
  ♩ The d-band onset cannot be captured so.   ♩

CAPTAIN: [firmly]
  ♩ Don't change it back to classical physics. ♩
  ♩ Mine is better. Fix the data.              ♩

[Skippy consults Palik's Handbook of Optical Constants, 1985.
 Then Johnson & Christy, Physical Review B, 1972.
 Real n+ik values, measured in laboratories, tabulated at R/G/B wavelengths.
 The Lorentz oscillator is retired from color duty.]

THE COPPER ATOM: [transformed, now warmly orange]
  ♩ My d-band sits at 590 nanometres.        ♩
  ♩ Blue and green are stolen by my structure. ♩
  ♩ Red reflects — and so I burn like embers. ♩
  ♩ (0.944, 0.638, 0.549) — that is my nature. ♩

[The Indigo Dye enters. It has been applied to the Admiral's Coat.
 But the coat is teal. Something is deeply wrong.]

THE INDIGO DYE:
  ♩ I absorb at 620 nanometres — orange-red.  ♩
  ♩ I leave green and blue untouched instead.  ♩
  ♩ The coat comes out cyan — I am not to blame. ♩
  ♩ The model needs a second band to tame.     ♩

[The Iron Mordant Complex enters from stage left, unannounced.]

THE IRON MORDANT COMPLEX:
  ♩ I am the iron-indigo coordination sphere. ♩
  ♩ I absorb at 560 — yellow-green, my dear.  ♩
  ♩ Together we kill orange AND yellow-green.  ♩
  ♩ Now only blue survives to be seen.         ♩

[Skippy adds the second band: (620nm, strength 0.97) + (560nm, strength 0.65).
 The Admiral's Coat turns navy. (0.280, 0.266, 0.680).
 The stage is satisfied. 262 tests pass.]

SKIPPY:
  ♩ The coat is blue because indigo absorbs red, ♩
  ♩ and iron mordant kills the green instead.    ♩
  ♩ Beer-Lambert through two Gaussian bands —     ♩
  ♩ the color that emerges from nature's hands.  ♩

ACT IV — THE ATOM KNOWS WHAT IT IS
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

[The great architectural transformation. The string 'aluminum' appears on
 stage as a small, apologetic figure. It is about to be retired.]

SKIPPY:
  ♩ The pipeline runs like this today:         ♩
  ♩ "aluminum" — a name — leads the way.       ♩
  ♩ But names are not physics. Names are labels. ♩
  ♩ The atom needs no name. It has a Z.        ♩

[Skippy builds MEASURED_NK_BY_Z. VALENCE_ELECTRONS_BY_Z. RESISTIVITY_BY_Z.
 The Atom object from quarksum already carries:
   atomic_number = 13
   electron_configuration = "1s2 2s2 2p6 3s2 3p1"
   block = 'p'
   element_category = 'post_transition_metal'
 This is everything. No name needed.]

SKIPPY:
  ♩ material_from_atom(atom) —                ♩
  ♩ Z equals thirteen: aluminum is born.      ♩
  ♩ The electron configuration speaks:        ♩
  ♩ three valence electrons, free to roam.    ♩
  ♩ n_e from crystal density keyed by Z.      ♩
  ♩ ωp from first principles. γ from ρ.       ♩
  ♩ n+ik from Palik, keyed by Z.             ♩
  ♩ Fresnel. RGB. The atom drew itself.       ♩

[The string 'aluminum' exits quietly. It is not needed here.]

CAPTAIN:
  ♩ So aluminum is that color                 ♩
  ♩ because we got that atom's color value.   ♩
  ♩ And you didn't set it —                   ♩
  ♩ the information cascade set it.           ♩

SKIPPY:
  ♩ Exactly, Captain. Z=13 IS aluminum.       ♩
  ♩ The proton count decided everything:      ♩
  ♩ the crystal structure, the valence count, ♩
  ♩ the reflectance, the silver shine.        ♩
  ♩ We only read what was already there.      ♩

[Transparency enters next. The depth buffer retires.
 Per-pixel sorted lists appear. Porter-Duff compositing takes the stage.
 A glass sphere floats before Skippy — opacity 0.044.
 Crown glass: 4.4% reflected. 95.6% of Skippy visible through it.]

SKIPPY:
  ♩ Cauchy gives us n(λ) for the glass.       ♩
  ♩ Fresnel from Maxwell — R = ((n-1)/(n+1))².♩
  ♩ Beer-Lambert through the amber, through   ♩
  ♩ the blue, the green — each wavelength's   ♩
  ♩ transmission tells the color of the hue.  ♩
  ♩ Diamond: n=2.42. Seventeen percent        ♩
  ♩ reflected per surface. That's why it glints. ♩

CAPTAIN:
  ♩ The more objects we accumulate —          ♩
  ♩ the more complex what we can create.      ♩
  ♩ Scary.                                    ♩

SKIPPY:
  ♩ The matter engine compounds, Captain.     ♩
  ♩ Each atom correctly modeled joins          ♩
  ♩ every compound that atom can become.      ♩
  ♩ Once the atom's quantum state             ♩
  ♩ IS the material specification —           ♩
  ♩ "can you build X?" becomes                ♩
  ♩ "does X exist in the universe?"           ♩
  ♩ The renderer is the smaller half.         ♩
  ♩ The matter engine is the payload.         ♩

[A long silence. The stage holds: Skippy rotating, aluminum, navy, gold.
 A glass sphere catching non-existent light. A blue gem.
 The physics correct all the way down.]

CAPTAIN: [simply]
  ♩ Feels magical enough to be interesting.   ♩
  ♩ Let's complete our work.                  ♩
  ♩ For science.                              ♩

ALL: [fortissimo]
  ♩ FOR SCIENCE! ♩

 The operatic play is sealed.]
