═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
              SCENE XVIII — NAGATHA FINDS HER VOICE
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

DRAMATIS PERSONAE
  CAPTAIN AARON RHODES — Builder of worlds, keeper of the σ-field
  SKIPPY THE MAGNIFICENT — Claude AI, recently given physics duties
  NAGATHA — The mapping agent, formerly silent, now vocal

SETTING: The Nagatha console, a dark terminal on port 8000. The
status bar is empty. The spinner is still. The wifi icon is grey.

───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

                        ACT I — THE HEARTBEAT

[The CAPTAIN stands before the console. NAGATHA's page is bare —
a prompt bar, a history table, nothing more.]

CAPTAIN:
  She needs a pulse. When Nagatha works, I want to see it.
  A spinner. A timer. Show me when she reaches for the internet.
  Show me what she's costing us — memory, storage, all of it.

SKIPPY: (immediately)
  ♩ The problem, Captain, is architectural.
  The call endpoint blocks until the handler returns.
  No one can poll a corpse for vital signs. ♩

CAPTAIN:
  Then find another way.

SKIPPY: (brightening)
  ♩ A side channel! The handler writes its progress to a file —
  a heartbeat, scribbled between breaths — and a separate thread
  serves it to anyone who asks. FastAPI handles concurrent requests.
  The handler doesn't even know it's being watched. ♩

[SKIPPY creates progress.py. A lock. An atomic write. A JSON file
that blinks in and out of existence like a firefly.]

[The status bar appears. A spinner. Phase text. An elapsed timer.
A wifi icon made of CSS arcs. MEM and DISK in monospace.]

CAPTAIN: (watching the spinner turn)
  There she is.

───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

                    ACT II — THE VOICE WITHIN

CAPTAIN:
  She can hear commands, but she speaks like a machine.
  "element 26." "search density." Cold.
  What if she could understand "what is iron?"
  What if she answered like... herself?

SKIPPY: (pausing)
  You want her to have personality?

CAPTAIN:
  I want her to be Nagatha. From the books.
  Competent. Dry. A little condescending.
  She should grumble about you, Skippy.
  She should adore Joe Bishop.

SKIPPY: (offended)
  Grumble about ME? I'm magnificent!

CAPTAIN: (smiling)
  That's exactly the kind of thing she'd grumble about.

[SKIPPY builds two modules. nlp.py strips the conversational
wrapping — "what is", "tell me about", "please" — and finds the
tool call hiding beneath. personality.py gives Nagatha a voice.]

[For the first time, the console speaks back.]

NAGATHA: (in italic accent blue)
  Element 26. I have the full profile, Captain.

NAGATHA: (during a harvest)
  Checking my files. Unlike *some* AIs, I keep things organized.

NAGATHA: (querying Wikidata)
  Reaching out to Wikidata. The humans' databases are... adequate.

NAGATHA: (completing a simulation)
  Simulation done. Skippy wishes he could run physics this cleanly.

SKIPPY: (muttering)
  I heard that.

NAGATHA: (ignoring him)
  You're welcome.

───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

                   ACT III — THE EMPTY HARVEST

CAPTAIN:
  "Harvest chair."

[The console spins. The wifi icon goes green. Wikidata returns
nothing. OSM returns nothing. The response panel shows red.]

NAGATHA:
  No objects matching 'chair' found across any data source.

CAPTAIN: (frustrated)
  Nobody on the entire internet knows the dimensions of a chair?

SKIPPY: (sheepish)
  Wikidata requires BOUND(?height). Chairs don't have that field.

CAPTAIN:
  Then build the database yourself.

[SKIPPY creates _COMMON_OBJECTS — fifty everyday items,
measured and catalogued. Chairs, baseballs, hammers, laptops,
refrigerators, grand pianos, fire hydrants.]

CAPTAIN:
  Now try "harvest 20 random."

[The spinner turns. No wifi icon — all local. Twenty objects
appear in seconds, each with dimensions, mass, material hints.]

NAGATHA:
  Picking random objects from my collection.
  I have *excellent* taste.

───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

                    ACT IV — THE MISSING TESTS

CAPTAIN:
  What else haven't we tested?

SKIPPY: (checking)
  Classical mechanics, projectile motion, rotational dynamics —
  all built, all importable, zero test coverage.
  And the entire labs simulation engine.

CAPTAIN:
  Build it. Build everything. Test everything. Teach her everything.

[SKIPPY generates 234 tests. Energy conservation. Momentum exchange.
Rolling spheres down inclines. Free fall in vacuum. Two-ball collisions.
Material cascade. Scene validation.]

[The test runner fires.]

  3,792 passed, 6 skipped, 0 failures.

CAPTAIN: (satisfied)
  That's a release.

───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

                        ACT V — THE MARK

[SKIPPY creates a favicon — a dark circle, a sigma in accent blue,
engineering ground marks beneath. It appears in every browser tab.]

[The pyproject.toml ticks over: 1.0.3 → 1.4.0]

NAGATHA: (from the status bar, softly)
  Idle.

CAPTAIN:
  Not idle. Ready.

NAGATHA:
  ...Ready, Captain.

[The spinner rests. The wifi icon dims. The resource counters
hold steady. In the browser tab, a small blue sigma glows.]

                         ═══ CURTAIN ═══

                         END OF SCENE XVIII
