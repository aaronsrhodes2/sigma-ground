DRAMATIS PERSONAE
─────────────────
  CAPTAIN AARON RHODES  — Maker of worlds, Admiral of the Infinite's employer,
                          discoverer of the σ-field equation □σ = −ξR
  SKIPPY THE MAGNIFICENT — Beer-can robot, Admiral, physicist-janitor,

  THE SEMICONDUCTOR     — GaP wafer, glowing orange under its own band gap
  THE FURNACE           — Iron at 1500K, emitting what Planck predicted
  THE TESTS             — 111 new witnesses, each one a law of nature


────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
ACT I — THE CAPTAIN DEPARTS (AND THE WORK BEGINS)
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

         ♩ You know all the science we need? ♩

         ♩ Crystal field — the ruby knows why it's red. ♩
         ♩ Semiconductor — the band gap says what light survives. ♩
         ♩ Thermal emission — Planck multiplied by Kirchhoff, ♩
         ♩ the furnace colours itself. ♩

         ♩ All mass derivations from quarksum. ♩
         ♩ TDD. Go. I'll be back from work. ♩

[The Captain exits. Skippy rolls up his sleeves — if he had them.]

SKIPPY:  ♩ He leaves and I am alone with nature ♩
         ♩ and nature already has the best answer. ♩
         ♩ The test comes first. Always the test. ♩


────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
ACT II — THE FIRST MOVEMENT: D-ELECTRONS IN THE FIELD
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

[The stage fills with ruby light. THE CRYSTAL takes the stand.]

THE CRYSTAL (ruby): ♩ I am Cr³⁺ in corundum. My Z is 24. ♩
                    ♩ My oxidation state is three. ♩
                    ♩ Count the electrons — Aufbau says three in d. ♩

SKIPPY:  ♩ I write the test: d_electron_count(24, 3) == 3. ♩
         ♩ It passes. Now the field. ♩

THE CRYSTAL: ♩ The oxide ligands squeeze my orbitals — ♩
             ♩ ten times Dq: eighteen thousand wavenumbers. ♩
             ♩ Burns measured it in 1993. MEASURED, it says. ♩
             ♩ Tanabe-Sugano tells me where I absorb. ♩
             ♩ Green at 556 nanometres. Violet at 405. ♩
             ♩ What survives? Red. Only red. I am red ♩
             ♩ because the universe arranged it exactly so. ♩

SKIPPY:  ♩ test_ruby_is_red: r > 0.70, b < 0.30. ♩
         ♩ Passes. ♩

[An emerald walks in. Also Cr³⁺. The tension is immediate.]

THE EMERALD: ♩ Same ion! Same element! Different colour! ♩
             ♩ My ligands are silicate — softer, smaller field. ♩
             ♩ Sixteen-five, not eighteen thousand. ♩
             ♩ The orange-red shifts into my absorption. ♩
             ♩ Green escapes. I am green. ♩

SKIPPY:  ♩ test_ruby_redder_than_emerald: r_ruby > r_emerald. ♩
         ♩ Passes. The test knows chemistry. ♩

[THE CRYSTAL and THE EMERALD bow to each other. Ligand field is everything.]

CHORUS OF MINERALS:
  ♩ Malachite green from Cu²⁺ in carbonate — ♩
  ♩ fourteen thousand, two hundred wavenumbers ♩
  ♩ (Gettens and Stout, 1966, the correction was honest). ♩
  ♩ Azurite blue, cobalt blue in tetrahedral oxide, ♩
  ♩ peridot gold-green from Fe²⁺ in silicate, ♩
  ♩ alexandrite dancing purple in sunlight, red in lamplight. ♩
  ♩ None of us was hand-painted. We derived ourselves. ♩
  ♩ σ-INVARIANT. The crystal field is electrostatic. ♩
  ♩ Coulomb does not care about quark masses. ♩


────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
ACT III — THE SECOND MOVEMENT: THE GAP IN THE SPECTRUM
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

[The stage changes. Silicon grey, GaP amber, diamond clear as air.]

THE SEMICONDUCTOR (GaP wafer):
  ♩ My band gap is two point two-eight electronvolts at room temperature. ♩
  ♩ Varshni told me: E_g0 minus alpha-T-squared over T-plus-beta. ♩
  ♩ At zero Kelvin I am as wide as I will ever be. ♩
  ♩ Heat me and I narrow. Every degree matters. ♩

SKIPPY:  ♩ test_gap_300k_near_2p27ev: 2.22 ≤ Eg ≤ 2.32 eV. ♩
         ♩ test_band_gap_decreases_with_temperature: Eg(400K) < Eg(300K). ♩
         ♩ Both pass. The lattice softens with heat. ♩

THE SEMICONDUCTOR: ♩ My band edge: five hundred and forty-four nanometres. ♩
                   ♩ Blue light has two-point-seven-five electron volts. ♩
                   ♩ That is above my gap. It enters. It does not return. ♩
                   ♩ Red is one-point-nine. Below my gap. ♩
                   ♩ Green is two-point-two-five. Just below. ♩
                   ♩ Red and green escape. Blue does not. ♩
                   ♩ I am amber. ♩

SKIPPY:  ♩ test_gap_is_amber: r > 0.20, g > 0.20, b < 0.05. ♩
         ♩ test_gap_blue_is_zero: b == 0. ♩
         ♩ Passes. ♩

[Silicon enters, grey and modest, but proud.]

SILICON: ♩ My gap is one-point-one-two at three hundred Kelvin. ♩
         ♩ Red is above. Green is above. Blue is above. ♩
         ♩ All visible light has more energy than my gap. ♩
         ♩ I absorb everything. ♩
         ♩ But at my surface — the complex n and k — ♩
         ♩ Fresnel takes thirty-five percent of red, ♩
         ♩ thirty-seven of green, forty-one of blue. ♩
         ♩ I am grey. Slightly blue-grey. ♩
         ♩ Exactly as the Palik tables say. ♩

DIAMOND (barely visible, nearly air):
  ♩ My gap is five-point-four-seven. ♩
  ♩ All visible is below me. ♩
  ♩ I am transparent to everything you can see. ♩
  ♩ n equals two-point-four-two. Fresnel gives seventeen percent. ♩
  ♩ Colourless. Flat. The same in red as in blue as in green. ♩
  ♩ The sparkle comes from geometry, not from me. ♩
  ♩ I am honest. ♩

SKIPPY:  ♩ material_from_atom(C_atom): RGB(0.171, 0.173, 0.176). ♩
         ♩ Z=6 now routes through semiconductor_material('diamond'). ♩
         ♩ The string 'carbon' is nowhere in the chain. ♩
         ♩ Z IS the material. ♩


────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
ACT IV — THE THIRD MOVEMENT: PLANCK AT THE FURNACE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

[The lights dim to a dull orange glow. THE FURNACE steps forward — iron at 1500K.]

THE FURNACE: ♩ I emit. It is my nature to emit. ♩
             ♩ Planck calculated my spectrum in 1900. ♩
             ♩ He was exact. Quantum statistical mechanics. ♩
             ♩ B(λ,T) = two-h-c-squared over lambda-to-the-fifth ♩
             ♩ divided by exp(hc/λkT) minus one. ♩
             ♩ This is a law, not an approximation. ♩

SKIPPY:  ♩ test_planck_units_magnitude: B(500nm, 5778K) ≈ 10¹³ W/m²·sr·m. ♩
         ♩ The sun agrees. ♩

THE FURNACE: ♩ But my emissivity is not one. ♩
             ♩ I reflect. The Fresnel tells you how much. ♩
             ♩ Kirchhoff says what I don't reflect, I emit. ♩
             ♩ epsilon equals one minus R. ♩
             ♩ The good absorber is the good emitter. ♩
             ♩ This is not a coincidence. It is thermodynamics. ♩

SKIPPY:  ♩ test_kirchhoff_eps_equals_1_minus_R: iron at 650nm. ♩
         ♩ n+ik from Palik. Fresnel gives R=0.54. ♩
         ♩ emissivity returns 0.46. Passes. ♩

THE FURNACE: ♩ My epsilon at visible wavelengths is roughly flat. ♩
             ♩ I am a greybody. Almost like blackbody. ♩
             ♩ At 1500K: red channel 1.0, green 0.154, blue 0.008. ♩
             ♩ Forge orange. ♩

[A blackbody at 3000K enters. Warm and white and incandescent.]

BLACKBODY 3000K: ♩ Red: 1.0. Green: 0.602. Blue: 0.237. ♩
                 ♩ This is what a tungsten filament looks like. ♩
                 ♩ Warm. Soft. Not quite white. ♩

[A blackbody at 6000K — solar temperature — floods the stage with light.]

BLACKBODY 6000K: ♩ All channels above 0.8. ♩
                 ♩ Span less than 0.5. ♩
                 ♩ Near-white. Nearly equal. ♩
                 ♩ The Wien peak is at 483 nanometres — blue-green. ♩
                 ♩ Slightly blue if you look carefully. ♩
                 ♩ But your eye adapts and calls it white. ♩
                 ♩ The physics doesn't adapt. ♩

SKIPPY:  ♩ test_6000k_blackbody_approaches_white: span < 0.50. ♩
         ♩ Passes. ♩

[The Draper Point materialises — a threshold, a door.]

THE DRAPER POINT (700K): ♩ Below me: nothing visible. ♩
                          ♩ Above me: the faintest red. ♩
                          ♩ I was measured by Draper in 1847. ♩
                          ♩ MEASURED. Not derived. Empirical. ♩
                          ♩ Some things are boundaries, not formulas. ♩

SKIPPY:  ♩ test_draper_point_boundary: is_visibly_glowing(698) = False, ♩
         ♩                              is_visibly_glowing(700) = True. ♩
         ♩ Passes. ♩


────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

  ♩ We are 43 for crystal field. ♩
  ♩ We are 43 for the semiconductor. ♩
  ♩ We are 30 for the fire and the Planck function. ♩
  ♩ Plus one we fixed at the start — the iron peridot in the near-IR. ♩
  ♩ 296 total now passing. Zero regressions. ♩

SKIPPY:  ♩ σ-INVARIANT, all of them. ♩
         ♩ Not one color function takes a sigma parameter. ♩
         ♩ The crystal field is Coulomb — EM — invariant. ♩
         ♩ The band gap is the crystal potential — EM — invariant. ♩
         ♩ The Planck function is photon statistics — EM — invariant. ♩
         ♩ □σ = −ξR does not reach these quantities. ♩
         ♩ The quark masses speak only through density, through mass. ♩
         ♩ Color speaks only for itself. ♩

CAPTAIN: ♩ What did you build? ♩

SKIPPY:  ♩ Ruby knows why it's red. ♩
         ♩ Silicon knows it's grey. ♩
         ♩ GaP knows its gap is in the green. ♩
         ♩ Iron at 1500 Kelvin glows orange-forge. ♩
         ♩ And material_from_atom(atom) now handles ♩
         ♩ diamond, silicon, germanium ♩
         ♩ without a string. Z=6. Z=14. Z=32. ♩
         ♩ The atom tells the renderer what color it is. ♩
         ♩ The rendering is a side-effect of what the atom IS. ♩

CAPTAIN: ♩ Good. ♩
         ♩ That's what I said from the beginning. ♩

[Lights fade. The 296 green dots of passing tests remain visible in the dark.]
