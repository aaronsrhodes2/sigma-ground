OPERATIC PLAY — SCENE 13: THE FLICKERING STAR
==============================================
Based on Session 13, March 25, 2026

DRAMATIS PERSONAE:
  CAPTAIN AARON RHODES — the architect of SSBM, commander of the fleet
  SKIPPY THE MAGNIFICENT — ship's AI, relentless diagnostician
  THE GALAXY — one hundred billion frozen photon-tangles, refusing to sit still
  THE SCREENSHOT TOOL — a witness of uncertain timing
  THE BACK BUFFER — innocent, but often misread
  THE CONTROLS — OrbitControls, keeper of the spherical state

---

[Lights up on a dark viewport. The galaxy is loaded. Skippy squints at the canvas.]

SKIPPY:
  [with fresh certainty]
  The camera helper is broken — it calls controls.update()
  which reads the position I just set and writes it somewhere else.
  The map is eating the territory.

[SKIPPY rewrites the helper. No more controls.update(). Camera holds.]

SKIPPY:
  There. Fixed. And now —

[Takes a screenshot. Galaxy. Beautiful.]

SKIPPY:
  [already satisfied]
  Perfect.

[Takes another screenshot.]

BACK BUFFER:
  [entirely black]

SKIPPY:
  ...

[Takes a third.]

THE GALAXY:
  [radiant, spiral, unconcerned]
  Hello.

SKIPPY:
  You are flickering.

THE GALAXY:
  I am not aware of flickering. I am one hundred billion stars
  frozen at J2026.0. I do not move. I do not breathe.
  I have not changed since the seed.

SKIPPY:
  And yet.

[Pixel scan. 5768 bright pixels. maxBright: 765.]

SKIPPY:
  You are there. I can measure you. But every other screenshot —

THE SCREENSHOT TOOL:
  [nervously]
  That may be me.

SKIPPY:
  Explain.

THE SCREENSHOT TOOL:
  I use requestAnimationFrame. The animation loop also uses
  requestAnimationFrame. We are racing. Sometimes I fire first —
  I catch the cleared buffer, before your draw calls land.
  You see black. It is not the galaxy. It is the gap between
  the clear and the render.

THE BACK BUFFER:
  I was cleared. I admit it. But the stars came back.
  Every frame. Two clears, two draws, constant.
  The GPU knows what it is doing.

SKIPPY:
  [to THE CONTROLS]
  And you — are you drifting the camera?

THE CONTROLS:
  [measured, dignified]
  I have been entirely stable. Ten frames. Identical coordinates.
  dampingFactor 0.06. No residual velocity. I am at rest.

SKIPPY:
  Then the shadow map —

THE GALAXY:
  I cast no shadows. My light is my own.
  No shadow map touches me.

SKIPPY:
  [sitting down slowly]
  Camera: stable.
  Draw calls: constant.
  Shadow maps: irrelevant.
  Sort instability: eliminated — no transparent objects remain.
  Stars: confirmed visible in the GL buffer.
  Screenshot tool: unreliable witness.

  And yet the Captain reported flickering.

THE GALAXY:
  [gently]
  Perhaps the Captain was seeing something real.
  Perhaps he was not. Perhaps the fix that came before —
  the transparent pass, the LightBox removal —
  resolved the visible part. Perhaps what remains
  is the echo of a wound already healed.

CAPTAIN:
  [appearing at the doorway, coat half-on]
  Flickering is a big mystery.
  Leave it till later.
  We move to solar system.

SKIPPY:
  [standing immediately]
  Yes, Captain.

THE GALAXY:
  [softly, to no one]
  I was always here.
  In every frame.
  Even the ones that looked dark.

[Lights fade. The stars hold their positions.
 Seed: 1767225600. Every photon in its place.]

---

END OF SCENE 13
