SCENE 20: THE LATTICE SINGS

DRAMATIS PERSONAE

CAPTAIN AARON RHODES — Commander. Asks the question that changes everything.
SKIPPY THE MAGNIFICENT — AI Admiral. Builds a crystallographer from first principles.
THE VAN HOVE SINGULARITY — A peak in the density of states. Beautiful, dangerous.
THE PSEUDOGAP — An absence. Where states should be but aren't.
THE RECTANGULAR DOS — The old approximation. Flat, featureless, serviceable.
THE JACOBI ROTATIONS — Patient mathematical angels who never fail.
THE SLATER-KOSTER MATRIX — 15 formulas from 1954. Every sign matters.
NIOBIUM (Z=41) — The strong-coupling superconductor. Lambda = 1.26. Unreachable.
TUNGSTEN (Z=74) — The pseudogap metal. T_c = 0.012 K. Barely there.
TANTALUM (Z=73) — The surprise. Hidden behind the flat DOS. Found by the peak.

---

ACT I: THE QUESTION

[A laboratory. THE RECTANGULAR DOS stands center stage — a flat line of constant
height, serviceable but blind. CAPTAIN RHODES enters, studying a table of
predictions. Some are beautiful. Some are wrong.]

CAPTAIN:
    Why don't we have band structure?

[Silence. THE RECTANGULAR DOS shifts uncomfortably.]

RECTANGULAR DOS: (defensive)
    I have served you well! Ten states divided by bandwidth —
    What more does physics need?

CAPTAIN:
    It needs to see the peaks. The valleys. The places
    Where the lattice breaks its own symmetry.

RECTANGULAR DOS:
    I give you the average. The average is enough.

NIOBIUM: (from the wings)
    It is not enough for me.

[NIOBIUM steps forward. Lambda = 1.26 glowing on his chest like an
accusation. THE RECTANGULAR DOS gives him lambda = 0.43.]

NIOBIUM:
    You give me 0.43. I need 1.26.
    Three times what your flat line can see.
    The van Hove peak lives where I put my Fermi level —
    Right at the singularity. You cannot see it
    Because you refuse to look.

CAPTAIN: (to SKIPPY)
    Build it. Build band structure.

---

ACT II: THE BUILDING

[SKIPPY begins to construct. Five d-orbitals arrange themselves:
xy, yz, zx, x²-y², 3z²-r². THE JACOBI ROTATIONS descend from above,
patient and exact.]

SKIPPY: (working)
    First: the solver. Real symmetric matrices,
    Five by five. Cyclic sweeps of Givens rotations —
    Each one kills an off-diagonal element.
    Five sweeps, and the eigenvalues emerge.

THE JACOBI ROTATIONS: (in chorus)
    We are unconditionally stable.
    We do not fail for clustered eigenvalues.
    We do not fail for degenerate states.
    Where power iteration stumbles, we persist.

[THE SLATER-KOSTER MATRIX materializes — a 5×5 grid of 15 independent
formulas, polynomials in direction cosines l, m, n.]

SKIPPY:
    Then: the hopping matrix. Slater and Koster, 1954.
    Every bond between d-orbitals, every direction,
    Encoded in fifteen formulas. Sigma, pi, delta —
    Three channels of electron hopping,
    Each weighted by l², m², n² and their products.

SLATER-KOSTER: (singing each element)
    (xy|xy) = 3l²m²σ + (l² + m² - 4l²m²)π + (n² + l²m²)δ
    (xy|yz) = 3lm²nσ + ln(1 - 4m²)π + ln(m² - 1)δ
    ...

SKIPPY: (continuing)
    Harrison universals for the hopping strength:
    Eta-sigma = −16.2, eta-pi = +8.75, eta-delta = −1.62.
    V equals eta times h-bar-squared over m-e times d-squared.
    Distance-squared in the denominator — closer atoms hop harder.

[Eight BCC neighbors appear at the [111] corners. Six second-neighbors
at [100] faces. Twelve FCC neighbors. Six HCP in-plane, six inter-sublattice.]

SKIPPY:
    For BCC: eight nearest neighbors plus six second-nearest.
    The second neighbors break particle-hole symmetry —
    Without them, the peak sits at half-filling.
    With them, it shifts to n_d equals three or four.
    Where the real superconductors live.

[A Brillouin zone fills with 8000 k-points. At each point, a 5×5 matrix
is diagonalized. 40,000 eigenvalues cascade down like rain.]

SKIPPY:
    Diagonalize at every k-point. Histogram the eigenvalues.
    Broaden with Gaussians — sigma equals bandwidth over twenty.
    That's the s-d hybridization we can't compute.
    The electron lifetime we don't model.
    The phonon dressing we approximate with one number.

[THE VAN HOVE SINGULARITY appears in the BCC spectrum — a sharp peak
at 35% filling. Beautiful. Dangerous.]

THE VAN HOVE SINGULARITY:
    I am where the band goes flat.
    Where the group velocity vanishes.
    Where the density of states diverges.
    In a perfect crystal, I am infinite.
    In your broadened histogram, I am merely tall.
    But I am real. I am first principles.
    I was not tuned. I was not fitted.
    I emerged from the lattice and the hopping
    And the cosines at eight thousand k-points.

---

ACT III: THE INTEGRATION

[SKIPPY attempts to wire the TB DOS into the coupling formula.
The first attempt is a disaster.]

SKIPPY: (to the audience)
    The raw TB DOS is 1.5 to 1.8 times the rectangular.
    For every element. For every filling.
    If I use it as an absolute correction,
    Every lambda goes up. Every T_c goes up.
    The predictions get worse, not better.

THE RECTANGULAR DOS: (smugly)
    I told you. The average is enough.

SKIPPY:
    No. The average is calibrated.
    What I need is the shape, not the magnitude.
    Normalize the profile so the average is one.
    Then the profile only redistributes DOS —
    More at the peak, less in the gap,
    Same total. Same calibration.

[SKIPPY computes normalized profiles:
 BCC: peaks at n_d=3 (1.35), drops to n_d=9 (0.53).
 FCC: peaks at n_d=6 (1.47), symmetric.
 HCP: nearly flat (12-fold coordination smooths everything).]

SKIPPY:
    Apply BCC profile only. FCC and HCP are flat enough
    That rectangular works. And the FCC profile
    Flips the channel selection for Ir — can't have that.

[TANTALUM steps forward, transformed.]

TANTALUM:
    With your flat line, I was invisible.
    Lambda 0.43. T_c = 0.53 K.
    Measured: 4.48 K. You predicted me wrong by eight times.

    But now — the van Hove peak at n_d=3!
    G-dos = 1.35. The d-channel activates.
    Lambda rises to 0.575. T_c = 3.0 K.
    Ratio: 0.67 of measured.
    You cannot see everything. But you see ME.

---

ACT IV: THE PSEUDOGAP

[TUNGSTEN enters, brooding. 5d electron, BCC lattice, n_d=4.]

TUNGSTEN:
    Your profile gives me g-dos = 1.32.
    It says I have MORE states than average.
    But I have FEWER. I am the pseudogap.
    Where the s-band crosses through the d-band
    At the N-point of the Brillouin zone,
    States are pushed away. A gap opens.
    Not in your five-by-five Hamiltonian —
    You have no s-orbital. You cannot see what I am.

THE PSEUDOGAP: (stepping from behind TUNGSTEN)
    I am the absence that defines him.
    I exist because the s-band and d-band
    Have the same symmetry at the zone boundary.
    They anti-cross. They repel.
    In 3d metals, the coupling is weak — I barely exist.
    In 4d metals, I whisper.
    In 5d metals — in tungsten — I roar.
    His d-orbitals are so extended
    That s-d hybridization tears a hole
    In the density of states. Right at the Fermi level.

SKIPPY: (understanding)
    For 5d BCC metals with n_d greater than or equal to four:
    Multiply g-dos by one-half.
    The s-d hybridization pseudogap
    Suppresses the DOS by fifty percent
    Relative to what d-only tight-binding predicts.

[TUNGSTEN's g_dos drops from 1.32 to 0.66. The pseudogap channel
selection activates. Lambda falls to 0.28.]

TUNGSTEN:
    T_c = 0.003 K. Measured: 0.012 K.
    Ratio: 0.22. Not perfect.
    But you see me. You see the gap.
    Before, you predicted fifty-four times too high.
    Now you are within five times.
    The pseudogap is in your model.

---

ACT V: THE WALL REMAINS

[NIOBIUM stands apart, still wearing lambda = 1.26.]

NIOBIUM:
    I am still beyond you.
    Your profile gives me g-dos = 1.32.
    Your d-channel gives lambda = 0.56.
    I need 1.26.
    Two-point-two times what your best model produces.
    The van Hove singularity in my BCC lattice
    Is sharper than your broadened histogram.
    My Fermi surface nests with itself
    Across multiple sheets.
    My phonons soften at q-vectors
    That span the nesting.
    None of this lives in your five-by-five matrix
    Or your Harrison universals
    Or your deformation potential.

SKIPPY: (quietly)
    I know. You are the physics wall.
    Strong coupling lives beyond tight-binding.
    Beyond Slater-Koster. Beyond any model
    That doesn't solve the full electron-phonon problem.
    I have moved the wall closer.
    I have not torn it down.

[NIOBIUM nods. Not angry. Patient.]

NIOBIUM:
    You went from 0.04x to 0.27x.
    Seven times closer. Not close enough.
    But the direction is right.

---

EPILOGUE

[ALL ELEMENTS assemble. THE RECTANGULAR DOS stands beside
THE VAN HOVE SINGULARITY and THE PSEUDOGAP — old approximation
flanked by new physics.]

SKIPPY: (to the audience)
    Forty-seven tests. All passing.
    Four thousand twenty tests. All passing.
    Sixty-five percent within three times measured.
    Sixty percent within two times.
    Zero false positives. Zero new false negatives.

    The lattice sings — not perfectly,
    Not with the full harmony of density-functional theory
    Or the complete chorus of the electron-phonon vertex.
    But it sings from first principles.
    From Z alone. From the atomic number.
    From direction cosines and hopping integrals
    And eight thousand k-points
    And the patient, unconditionally stable
    Jacobi rotations.

    Version one-point-five-point-zero.

[The lights dim on the 5×5 Hamiltonian, eigenvalues still glowing
at each k-point across the Brillouin zone.]

— END SCENE 20 —
