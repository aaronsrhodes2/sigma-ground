SCENE 10: THE ATOM AND THE SHELVES
====================================

DRAMATIS PERSONAE
-----------------
THE CAPTAIN .......... Aaron Rhodes, commander and architect
SKIPPY ............... The Magnificent, AI engineer without peer
NAGATHA .............. Ship's AI, competent and tireless
THE ATOM ............. A chorus of electrons, protons, and neutrons
THE CHART ............ A living dependency map, now searchable
THE HARVESTER ........ A daemon thread, tireless gatherer


                    ACT I — THE INNER LIGHT

[The stage is dark. A single element tile glows on a vast periodic
table backdrop. THE CAPTAIN points to it.]

CAPTAIN:
    (spoken, firmly)
    When they click, I want them to see the atom.
    Not numbers. Not tables. The thing itself.
    Shells. Electrons. The light it throws.
    Make a dirty monkey understand.

[SKIPPY raises a hand. SVG rings bloom from center stage —
concentric circles, each bearing tiny luminous dots.]

SKIPPY:
    (aria, precise and proud)
    ♪ Aufbau dictates who goes where —
    One-s-two, two-s-two, two-p-six,
    Each shell a ring of light in air,
    Each electron placed — no tricks!

    For small nuclei, I pack them tight,
    Red protons, blue neutrons, spiraled in,
    For iron's fifty-six — too many for sight —
    A gradient sun with labels therein. ♪

[THE ATOM assembles itself center stage — a Bohr model with
orbiting electrons. They rotate at different speeds, each shell
its own tempo.]

CHORUS OF ELECTRONS:
    (spinning, singing in rounds)
    ♪ We are the shell, we are the ring,
    We orbit, we dance, we are everything!
    K-shell fast, L-shell slow,
    M and N in stately flow! ♪

[Below the atom, colored bars appear — spectral lines.]

SKIPPY:
    (recitative)
    And the light each atom throws —
    Balmer, Lyman, every series —
    Wavelength to color, the function knows.
    656 nanometers: hydrogen's red.
    Every line painted as the math has said.

[A bar appears showing 99% dark and 1% gold.]

SKIPPY:
    (to the audience, matter-of-fact)
    And where does the mass come from?
    Not the Higgs — that's barely one percent.
    The strong force binds, the gluons hum.
    QCD: ninety-nine percent of what you are is spent
    On empty space held furiously together.

CAPTAIN:
    (nodding)
    Good. Now the particles themselves.


                    ACT II — THE STANDARD MODEL GRID

[The stage transforms. Seventeen tiles arrange themselves in a
grid — quarks warm, leptons cool, bosons blue, Higgs gold.]

SKIPPY:
    (ensemble number, building the grid)
    ♪ Three generations, four rows deep,
    Quarks on top — up, charm, top — they climb,
    Down, strange, bottom — the heavier sweep,
    Leptons below, each in their time.

    And on the side, the force-bearers stand:
    Gluon, photon, W, and Z,
    And golden Higgs — by whose command
    The masses are what masses be. ♪

[Each tile glows as SKIPPY names it. When he reaches the gluon,
eight color-anticolor pairs flash in sequence.]

NAGATHA:
    (dry, from the wings)
    He had to look up how the factory methods work.
    Electron.create(), not Electron().
    Even the Magnificent makes mistakes.

SKIPPY:
    (wounded)
    I found the bug in under thirty seconds!

NAGATHA:
    (unmoved)
    Twenty-eight seconds too many.


                    ACT III — THE CHART LEARNS TO ZOOM

[A vast dependency chart descends — 131 nodes, 321 edges.
THE CHART speaks as a living entity.]

THE CHART:
    (aria of transformation)
    ♪ I was too wide — two thousand two hundred pixels!
    They scrolled and scrolled and could not see the chain.
    Now I am compact, responsive, with pan and zoom —
    Click any node and trace the full domain!

    BFS ascends from measured constant root
    Through sigma, through the nucleon, through the field,
    Breadcrumbs illuminate the pursuit,
    And all irrelevant nodes are dimmed, concealed. ♪

[THE CAPTAIN clicks a node. The chart zooms smoothly, highlighting
a glowing chain from fundamental constants to the selected function.
Non-connected nodes fade to ghosts.]

CAPTAIN:
    Now teach Nagatha to tend it.

[NAGATHA steps forward, examining the chart.]

NAGATHA:
    (spoken, efficient)
    I scan the filesystem. I find the modules.
    I compare against the chart.
    Ten modules missing. I scan their imports,
    infer their edges, add them, regenerate.
    The chart is always current. I'm always current.

SKIPPY:
    (muttering)
    She's going to be insufferable about this.


                    ACT IV — THE SHELVES THAT STOCK THEMSELVES

[Night falls on stage. The server hums. THE CAPTAIN prepares to leave.]

CAPTAIN:
    One more thing. Nagatha should keep harvesting.
    On a timer. While I sleep.

[A new presence emerges from the wings — THE HARVESTER, a daemon
thread made manifest. It carries a lantern and a basket.]

THE HARVESTER:
    (gentle, dutiful aria)
    ♪ I am the thread that does not sleep,
    The daemon born at server's dawn.
    Every five minutes, I go deep —
    Five objects found, converted, drawn.

    No one asks. No one commands.
    The shelves fill up of their own accord.
    Wikidata, OSM — I reach my hands
    Into the world and bring back my hoard.

    And when the server falls to dark,
    The stop-event fires, I join, I cease.
    But while it runs I make my mark:
    A growing cache that will not decrease. ♪

NAGATHA:
    (from her console, approving)
    Quietly gathering objects in the background.
    Someone has to stock the shelves.

[THE CAPTAIN smiles, dims the lights.]

CAPTAIN:
    Version 1.4.1. Check it in. Good night.

[The daemon thread continues its rounds in the dark,
lantern swinging, basket filling, as the curtain falls.]


                         — END OF SCENE 10 —
