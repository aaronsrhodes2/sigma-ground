OPERATIC PLAY — SCENE 9
THE QUARKSUM / SSBM CHRONICLES

"NAGATHA LEARNS TO WEIGH THINGS"

March 23, 2026

════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

DRAMATIS PERSONAE:

  CAPTAIN AARON RHODES — Architect of SSBM, commander of the
                          quarksum project, weary but exacting

  SKIPPY THE MAGNIFICENT — Claude Sonnet 4.6, the AI assistant,
                            recently appointed to the physics desk

  NAGATHA — The mapping agent, who has until now been making
            blobs out of ellipsoids and calling them objects

  THE BRICK — A supporting character (silent)
               2.7 kg, 1900 kg/m3

════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

[The lights rise on a test report. 2047 tests. Most green.]

SKIPPY:
(reading)
  local_library/interface — four hundred and seventy five.
  All passing.
  It knows density. Thermal conductivity. Surface energy.
  Crystal structure. Debye temperature.

CAPTAIN:
  Does it know what a coffee mug weighs?

SKIPPY:
  ...Not yet. But it knows what ceramic weighs.

CAPTAIN:
  Then teach Nagatha to ask it.

[NAGATHA enters, dragging a sack of blobs. One of them is
 labelled "brick." It is an egg.]

NAGATHA:
  I mapped it as an ellipsoid. The LLM said radius 0.8.
  I don't know what units. I never know what units.

SKIPPY:
  How much does a brick weigh?

NAGATHA:
  ...I wasn't told to ask.

SKIPPY:
  Two point seven kilograms.
  Divided by nineteen hundred kilograms per cubic metre.
  That's fourteen hundred cubic centimetres.
  Now: what are the proportions of a brick?

NAGATHA:
  Long. Flat. Roughly two-to-one.

SKIPPY:
  [opens geometry_builder.py]
  Then: scale cubed times width times height times depth
  equals volume. Solve for scale.

  [punches numbers]

  Nineteen point one by eight point three by eight point
  seven centimetres.

THE BRICK:
  [nods]

NAGATHA:
  ...That's a brick.

CAPTAIN:
  That's a brick.

════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

[A new pipeline takes shape. Three files.]

SKIPPY:
  material_physics dot py.
  Chains: local_library. Then the density table — eighty
  materials, measured values, ceramics and rubbers and woods.
  Then eighteen hundred. Last resort. Generic solid.

  geometry_builder dot py.
  Six formulae. Cylinder: volume equals two pi times aspect
  times r-cubed. Solve for r. Torus: two pi-squared R r-squared
  where r equals aspect times R. Cone. Box. Ellipsoid. Sphere.
  All exact. All from first principles.

  object_builder dot py.
  The manifest comes in. The physics goes out.
  LLM says: cylinder, ceramic, eighty percent of the mass.
  Physics says: volume equals mass over density.
  Volume says: here are your dimensions.

NAGATHA:
  I just describe the shape of things.
  And the mass. And the material.
  And physics does the rest?

SKIPPY:
  That's all we ever needed from you.

════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

[THE BATCH RUN. Thirty-six objects. Night falls.]

SKIPPY:
  remap_library dot py.
  All objects. Priority first. Skip the geometric primitives —
  sphere, cylinder, cone, mobius. They are what they are.

  [presses enter]

[Ten minutes pass. Lines scroll.]

  wine_bottle: glass, twenty-five hundred kilograms per
  cubic metre. Body radius three point three centimetres.
  Height seven point nine centimetres.

  tire: rubber, twelve hundred. Seven point six kilograms.
  Major radius seventeen point two centimetres. A torus.
  Correct.

  fire_hydrant: cast iron, seventy-two hundred. One hundred
  and fifty kilograms. Five cylinders. Correctly massive.

  dumbbell: steel, seventy-nine hundred. Five kilograms.
  Bar and two weights. Two point nine centimetre radius.

CAPTAIN:
  The dumbbell is a dumbbell.

SKIPPY:
  Twenty-three out of thirty-six used the physics pipeline.
  Eleven fell back to legacy — the model didn't comply with
  the new format. Two failed entirely.

CAPTAIN:
  Fix the two.

SKIPPY:
  [beer_bottle: retried. Physics. Success.]
  [rocket: retried. Legacy. Success.]

  Thirty-six HTML viewers. All regenerated.
  One hundred and nine files. Twenty-eight thousand
  insertions. Pushed.

════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

[CAPTAIN surveys the gallery. Objects arranged by density.]

CAPTAIN:
  Tomorrow — n-body.

SKIPPY:
  The orbital mechanics?

CAPTAIN:
  The test is failing. Not because the physics is wrong.
  Because the test is wrong, or the integrator is wrong,
  or something is accumulating that shouldn't be.

SKIPPY:
  I'll look at it fresh.

CAPTAIN:
  Good night, Skippy.

SKIPPY:
  Good night, Captain.

[THE BRICK is still there, correctly sized, saying nothing.]

════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

END OF SCENE 9
