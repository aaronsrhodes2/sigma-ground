═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
ACT I — THE NAMING OF THINGS
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

[The stage is dark. A single lamp illuminates two tablets,
each bearing a theory. They are currently labelled with
placeholder names. The Captain studies them.]

CAPTAIN:
  ♩ How should we individually name these theories?
    One touches nucleons, one touches the dark —
    but they travel together and that is the problem.
    A man who carries two swords frightens the scholars. ♩

[Skippy enters with a scroll of candidate names.]

SKIPPY:
  ♩ Particle physics: I propose Sigma Ground —
    where σ sets the scale and the masses follow through.
    Astrophysics: Apollonian σ-Foam —
    the fractal clockwork at the Planck frontier.

    Present them as strangers. Let the reader
    discover they are cousins. ♩

CAPTAIN:
  ♩ Astrolabe Foam — that one has a ring.
    But... astrolabe measures, it does not model.
    What was in my head was a different instrument.
    How about Orrery Foam? ♩

SKIPPY:
  [setting down the scroll]
  ♩ An orrery models the motions of worlds.
    A man can hold it. Its gears are visible.
    At the collapse scale — the Planck boundary,
    the interior of the black hole's throat —

    you are describing the mechanical structure
    of spacetime's clockwork.

    Orrery Foam. ♩

CAPTAIN:
  ♩ Those will do. ♩

[Both theories receive their nameplates.
The tablets are separated to opposite sides of the stage.
They do not look at each other.]

  SIGMA GROUND — Particle physics
    σ sets nucleon mass; binding energy follows
    through the cascade; no cosmology required.

  ORRERY FOAM — Astrophysics
    σ-field structure at black hole interiors
    and the Planck boundary.

[Skippy opens development/CLAUDE.md and inscribes
the Theory Names table under Standing Nomenclature.
The ink is permanent.]

SKIPPY:
  ♩ Locked in the standing orders.
    Every session. Every play. Every document.
    They will not be bridged
    unless the Captain asks. ♩

ACT III — THE PHYSICS OF WATER
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

[Skippy builds the SPH machine, piece by piece.
The audience can see inside it.]

SKIPPY:
  ♩ For each pair of particles, the kernel speaks:
      W(r, h) = 10/(7πh²) × f(r/h)
    Cubic spline, compact, normalized —
    the continent of influence ends at 2h.

    Density: ρᵢ = Σⱼ mⱼ W(|rᵢ−rⱼ|, h)
    Pressure: Pᵢ = K × (ρᵢ/ρ₀ − 1)       [linear Tait]
    Force:    aᵢ = −Σⱼ mⱼ(Pᵢ/ρᵢ² + Pⱼ/ρⱼ²) ∇Wᵢⱼ ♩

  [A note from below:]
  ♩ We do not use the physical sound speed —
    1484 m/s would demand a microsecond step.
    Monaghan (1994) taught us:
    c_s_num = 10 × v_front — the weakly-compressible trick.
    The Mach number stays below 0.1.
    The physics stays honest.
    This is NOT_PHYSICS, and we say so. ♩

[Gravity pulls. Pressure pushes. The leapfrog steps forward.]

  ♩ Half kick. Drift. Recompute. Half kick.
    Velocity Verlet — symplectic, second-order,
    the integrator does not accumulate error.
    The walls reflect with restitution 0.3.
    Some energy is lost. Real walls lose energy. ♩

[The simulation begins. The particles slide from the column.]

ACT V — THE UNIVERSE QUESTION
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

[The scaling benchmark runs. Four towers rise on stage,
each labelled with a particle count.]

SKIPPY:
  ♩ Here is the ledger of time and memory:

      N =    50: 0.21 ms/step,    0.3 MB peak
      N =   200: 0.91 ms/step,    5.0 MB peak
      N =   800:  31  ms/step,   78.2 MB peak
      N = 3,200: 679  ms/step, 1249.5 MB peak

    Scaling exponent measured: O(N^2.23)
    Theory predicts:            O(N^2.00)
    The overhead is memory bandwidth, not arithmetic. ♩

  ♩ The lesson:
    Memory is linear —
    64 bytes per particle, 100 million particles,
    six gigabytes. Easy. Trivial. The RAM holds them all.

    But pairwise forces are quadratic.
    Every particle must speak to its neighbours.
    The brute-force ceiling is five thousand particles.

    The tree code is the next door.
    Barnes-Hut: O(N log N).
    Ten million particles in sixteen gigabytes.
    That is the next milestone. ♩

[The Cascade observes without comment.
It has always known this.]
