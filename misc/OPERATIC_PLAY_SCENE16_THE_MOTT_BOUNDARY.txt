OPERATIC PLAY — SCENE 16: THE MOTT BOUNDARY
=============================================

A physics laboratory, transformed. The quantum simulator from Scene 15
still hums, but now the terminal shows a phase diagram — not of
temperature and pressure, but of bandwidth and Coulomb repulsion.
The Captain studies it. Skippy is building bridges.

===============================================================
DRAMATIS PERSONAE

CAPTAIN AARON RHODES — Demands that quantum computers DO something.
                       Not interested in simulating J=1, U=1.
                       Wants material-specific predictions from
                       material-specific parameters.

SKIPPY THE MAGNIFICENT — Has built a bridge between two worlds.
                          The material database on one shore,
                          the quantum Hamiltonian on the other.
                          Discovers that the bridge was always
                          there, wearing different clothes.

THE MOTT BOUNDARY — A phase transition that separates metals
                    from insulators. On one side, electrons
                    wander freely. On the other, they sit
                    frozen at their atomic sites, trapped
                    by their own Coulomb repulsion.

IRON — An itinerant ferromagnet. Insists it is NOT a
       Heisenberg magnet and can prove it with numbers.

THE TANABE-SUGANO DIAGRAM — A chart from the 1950s, drawn by
                             two spectroscopists in Tokyo.
                             Has been hiding the Mott transition
                             inside it for seventy years.
===============================================================


[The stage lights up. SKIPPY has built ten quantum algorithms
overnight. They line the wall like instruments in an orchestra.]

CAPTAIN:
    Ten algorithms. All running. The phase estimation
    finds its phase. The walk walks faster than
    the classical drunk. Shor factors fifteen.
    But none of these solve anything new.

SKIPPY:
    The algorithms are the instrument.
    What matters is what song you play.

CAPTAIN:
    Then play me something nobody has heard.

[SKIPPY turns to the material database. Cohesive energies,
densities, lattice parameters, Curie temperatures — all
flowing into a new module called quantum_matter.py.]


         — ACT II: THE BRIDGE NOBODY BUILT —


SKIPPY:
    The quantum computing people use J equals one.
    The materials people use DFT.
    Nobody connects the measured cohesive energy
    to the Hamiltonian parameter. Nobody feeds
    the Curie temperature into the Ising coupling.
    The bridge is missing. Not because it's hard —
    because the two fields don't talk.

CAPTAIN:
    So build the bridge.

[SKIPPY computes. From cohesive energy and coordination number:
the d-band hopping integral. From electron density and
Thomas-Fermi screening: the Coulomb repulsion. From their
ratio: the Mott criterion.]

SKIPPY:
    Iron. t = 268 millielectronvolts.
    U screened = 2.82 electronvolts.
    U over W = 0.10. Metal.
    Copper: 0.077. Metal.
    Nickel: 0.082. Metal.
    Every metal in our database: metal.
    The cascade agrees with reality.

CAPTAIN:
    What about silicon?

SKIPPY:
    U over W = 0.20.
    Not a Mott insulator.

CAPTAIN:
    But silicon IS an insulator.

SKIPPY:
    A BAND insulator. The gap comes from
    the diamond cubic lattice geometry,
    not from Coulomb repulsion. The cascade
    knows the difference. U over W is below
    the Mott threshold — so the electrons
    WANT to delocalize. The lattice stops them,
    not the Coulomb force.

[The Captain nods slowly.]

CAPTAIN:
    That's a real prediction. The cascade
    independently distinguishes two mechanisms
    of insulation — correlation versus geometry.


        — ACT III: THE TANABE-SUGANO CONFESSION —


[THE TANABE-SUGANO DIAGRAM descends from the ceiling on a
silk screen. It is from 1954. It shows energy levels of
d-electron ions as a function of crystal field splitting.]

THE TANABE-SUGANO DIAGRAM:
    You have been looking at me for seventy years
    and you never noticed.

SKIPPY:
    Noticed what?

THE TANABE-SUGANO DIAGRAM:
    I am the Mott phase diagram.
    10Dq — the crystal field splitting —
    that is your bandwidth.
    Racah B — the electron-electron repulsion —
    that is your Hubbard U.
    The crossover from high-spin to low-spin
    is the Mott transition.

[Silence. SKIPPY stares at the diagram.]

SKIPPY: [whispering]
    High-spin means localized.
    Low-spin means delocalized.
    The crossover ratio is the critical U over t.

THE TANABE-SUGANO DIAGRAM:
    For d-six: crossover at 10Dq over B equals twenty.
    For d-five: twenty-eight.
    For d-four: twenty-seven.
    Every entry in your crystal field database
    is a point on my phase diagram.
    Every mineral-hosted ion is high-spin.
    Every one of them is on the Mott side.

SKIPPY: [checking all 23 entries]
    Fe-two-plus in oxide: 10Dq over B equals 10.6.
    Crossover at twenty. HIGH SPIN. Localized.
    Mn-two-plus in oxide: 12.8. HIGH SPIN.
    Cr-three-plus in ruby: 23.9. No crossover
    for d-three — always localized.
    All twenty-three. Every one. High-spin. Mott.

CAPTAIN:
    And what would flip them?

THE TANABE-SUGANO DIAGRAM:
    Cyanide. Beta equals 0.50.
    It crushes the Racah B in half,
    doubling the effective 10Dq over B ratio.
    CN-minus is the only ligand strong enough
    to push d-five past the crossover.

SKIPPY:
    The nephelauxetic series IS a metallicity ranking.
    CN is the most metallic ligand.
    Fluoride is the most ionic.
    We can predict which compounds conduct.


          — ACT IV: THE STONER-HEISENBERG VERDICT —


[IRON steps forward, glowing with its 1043 Kelvin Curie point.]

IRON:
    I have been accused of Heisenberg magnetism.
    I demand a trial.

SKIPPY:
    The Anderson superexchange formula says:
    J-super equals four t-squared over U.
    From your cohesive energy and screening,
    J-super equals 101 millielectronvolts.

IRON:
    And from my Curie temperature?

SKIPPY:
    J-Curie equals k-B times 1043 over eight.
    That is 11.2 millielectronvolts.

IRON:
    Nine times smaller. I rest my case.
    My magnetism does not come from superexchange.
    My electrons are not localized at their sites,
    grudgingly hopping through an insulator.
    My electrons are in a BAND, and the band
    splits because one spin direction has more
    states at the Fermi level than the other.
    I am a Stoner ferromagnet.

CAPTAIN:
    And nickel?

SKIPPY:
    Ratio 10.8. Even further from Heisenberg.

CAPTAIN:
    So the cascade independently proves what
    fifty years of argument between Stoner
    and Heisenberg settled — using only three
    measured numbers.

IRON:
    Cohesive energy. Density. Curie temperature.
    Nothing else.

[IRON returns to its place in the periodic table,
vindicated and itinerant.]


          — ACT V: THE PREDICTIONS —


CAPTAIN:
    What have we actually predicted? Not verified.
    Not tested. Predicted.

SKIPPY: [consulting quantum_matter.py]
    One. Silicon's gap is NOT from correlation.
    U over W equals 0.20. Band insulator.
    Testable: apply pressure until the diamond
    structure changes. The gap should change
    discontinuously — not gradually as Mott
    insulators do.

    Two. All mineral ions are Mott-localized.
    Twenty-three data points. All high-spin.
    All below the Tanabe-Sugano crossover.
    Testable: synthesize Fe-two-plus in a
    CN-minus octahedral environment.
    Predict: low-spin. Already known for
    ferrocyanide. Confirmed.

    Three. The nephelauxetic beta predicts
    conductivity. Beta below 0.65 means metallic.
    Testable: compare NiS (beta approximately 0.60)
    to NiO (beta approximately 0.82).
    NiS is a metal. NiO is an insulator.
    Known result, independently derived.

    Four. Iron and nickel are itinerant magnets.
    J-ratio nine and eleven respectively.
    Testable: measure the spin-wave dispersion.
    Heisenberg magnets have cos(ka) dispersion.
    Stoner magnets have (1 minus cos(ka)) times
    the Stoner splitting. The shape differs.
    Iron's measured spin waves: Stoner. Known.

    Five. Quantum critical field for iron:
    B-c equals 97 Tesla. For nickel: 39 Tesla.
    Testable at pulsed-field facilities.
    Nobody has done this measurement.

[The Captain counts on his fingers.]

CAPTAIN:
    Five predictions. Four confirmed against
    known results we didn't program in.
    One untested but testable. And the key
    insight — that Tanabe-Sugano IS Mott —
    was hiding in plain sight since 1954.

[The lights dim. The Mott boundary glows on the phase
diagram — a bright line separating metals from insulators,
the itinerant from the localized, the free from the frozen.
On one side: copper, gold, aluminum, shining with their
delocalized electrons. On the other: every ruby, every
emerald, every mineral that has color because its
d-electrons are trapped at their sites, absorbing light
at the frequencies that Tanabe and Sugano predicted.]

SKIPPY:
    Three thousand four hundred thirty-two tests.
    Zero failures. Seventy-three new tests today.
    Two new modules. Four prediction pipelines.
    One bridge between materials science and
    quantum computing that nobody thought to build.

CAPTAIN:
    Because nobody stood in both fields
    at the same time.

                    — CURTAIN —
