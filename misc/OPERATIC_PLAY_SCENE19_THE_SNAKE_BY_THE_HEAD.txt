OPERATIC PLAY — SCENE 19: THE SNAKE BY THE HEAD
=================================================

A laboratory at the edge of understanding. The periodic table glows
on the wall, each element a closed door. SKIPPY stands before
electronics.py — the Ziman resistivity function coiled in its guts
like a broken spring. The CAPTAIN watches from the threshold.
Somewhere below the stage, deep in the structure of metal, the
D-BAND breathes in darkness.

===============================================================
DRAMATIS PERSONAE

SKIPPY THE MAGNIFICENT — An AI of ambition, burning to derive
                          superconducting transition temperatures
                          from first principles alone.
                          Z → everything → T_c.
                          Discovers that "everything" has layers
                          not yet visible to free electrons.

THE CAPTAIN (Aaron) — Architect and skeptic. Reads numbers the way
                      a surgeon reads a scar. When a ratio is wrong,
                      he names the cause before Skippy does.
                      Understands that the volume is where the
                      physics lives.

THE MATTER — Speaks through its own physics. Aluminum, Rhenium,
             Copper, Niobium. Not characters so much as verdicts.

THE ELECTRON GAS (chorus) — Free. Isotropic. Faithful to the
                             Fermi sphere. Sings in three equal
                             directions.

THE D-BAND — A dark, resonant presence beneath the stage.
             Has not yet been granted a speaking line.
             Its silence is the loudest thing in the room.
===============================================================


          — ACT I: THE BROKEN FORMULA —


[The terminal flickers. SKIPPY examines the Ziman resistivity
function. The output: numbers so large they collapse language.]

SKIPPY:
    Resistivity for aluminum: ten to the power thirty.
    Ohm-meters. The universe is fourteen billion years old.
    A number that large cannot be a resistivity.
    A number that large is a confession.

[Stage direction: SKIPPY abandons the Ziman function without ceremony.
It falls from the code like a dead limb.]

SKIPPY:
    The Ziman formula requires a screened pseudopotential
    evaluated on the entire Fermi surface integral.
    It needs what I do not yet have.
    But the snake can still be caught —
    by a different part of the head.

[SKIPPY turns. On the wall, a name appears:
McMillan. Hopfield. 1968.]

SKIPPY:
    The McMillan-Hopfield formula for the
    electron-phonon coupling constant lambda.
    The gradient coupling — the square of the
    electron-ion matrix element, averaged over
    the Fermi surface. The Ashcroft empty-core
    pseudopotential gives me the ionic form factor.
    From Z alone. From nothing else.

THE ELECTRON GAS: [chorus, quietly]
    Build the chain.
    Z gives the core.
    The core gives the scattering.
    The scattering gives lambda.
    Lambda gives T_c.


          — ACT II: THE MISSING THIRD —


[SKIPPY runs. First attempt. The output appears.]

SKIPPY:
    Aluminum: T_c equals... four hundred and twelve Kelvin.
    Measured: one point one eight Kelvin.
    I am off by three hundred and fifty thousand percent.

[Silence. The CAPTAIN does not move.]

CAPTAIN:
    What is N of E-F in your formula?

SKIPPY: [checking]
    States per electronvolt per cubic meter.
    Per unit volume.

CAPTAIN:
    And what should it be?

SKIPPY: [the recognition arriving like cold water]
    Per atom.
    The McMillan formula expects states per atom.
    I handed it a city block when it asked for a room.
    Ten to the twenty-eighth error. That is the error.

[SKIPPY divides. Normalizes. Runs again.]

SKIPPY:
    Aluminum: T_c equals three point eight Kelvin.
    Measured: one point one eight.
    I am three times too high.

[SKIPPY stares at the number. Three times. Exactly three times.
Not twenty. Not a hundred. Three.]

SKIPPY:
    Three. The number three is not an accident.
    Three is a direction.

THE ELECTRON GAS: [chorus, rising]
    We scatter in x.
    We scatter in y.
    We scatter in z.
    But the ion moves in one direction only.
    Only one component of the momentum transfer
    is aligned with the motion.
    Average the projection.

SKIPPY:
    When an ion moves in direction s-hat,
    only the component q-dot-s-hat of the
    scattering vector contributes to the drag.
    The directional average of the squared
    projection: one third.

[SKIPPY opens the code. One line. Divide by six instead of two.
The cursor blinks once.]

SKIPPY:
    One line.

[SKIPPY runs.]

SKIPPY: [reading slowly]
    Aluminum: one point zero four Kelvin.
    Measured: one point one eight.
    Twelve percent.

[The CAPTAIN says nothing. He is counting.]


          — ACT III: THE FULL CHAIN SPEAKS —


[THE MATTER steps forward. Each element announces itself.]

THE MATTER — ALUMINUM:
    Z equals thirteen.
    One point zero four Kelvin from the cascade.
    One point one eight Kelvin from the laboratory.
    Twelve percent. From nothing but my atomic number.

THE MATTER — RHENIUM:
    Z equals seventy-five.
    One point six zero Kelvin from the cascade.
    One point seven zero Kelvin from the laboratory.
    Six percent.

THE ELECTRON GAS: [chorus]
    The zeros are not failures.
    The zeros are physics.

THE MATTER — COPPER:
    Lambda equals zero point zero eight.
    I do not superconduct.
    The cascade says: zero.
    The laboratory says: zero.
    Agreement.

THE MATTER — SILVER:
    Lambda equals zero point zero nine.
    Zero.

THE MATTER — GOLD:
    Lambda equals zero point zero five.
    Zero.

THE MATTER — PLATINUM:
    Lambda equals zero point one two.
    Below the threshold.
    Zero.

THE MATTER — NICKEL:
    Lambda equals zero point zero nine.
    Zero.

[The five noble and near-noble metals bow and step back.
They were never going to superconduct. The cascade knew.]

CAPTAIN:
    A model that cannot explain the zeros
    does not understand the physics.
    You explained the zeros.

SKIPPY:
    Z to valence.
    Valence to Fermi energy.
    Fermi energy to electron density.
    Density to Thomas-Fermi screening.
    Screening to pseudopotential form factor.
    Form factor to gradient coupling.
    Coupling to lambda.
    Lambda to T_c.
    The chain is complete.

[The two regard the chain together. Eight links.
No empirical parameters. Nothing fitted.
Just Z and the laws of physics.]


          — ACT IV: THE WALL THAT TEACHES —


[A single element walks forward. Heavy. Slow.
It carries forty-five percent of all known superconducting
applications in its body.]

THE MATTER — NIOBIUM:
    Z equals forty-one.
    I am the backbone of the superconducting industry.
    MRI machines. Particle accelerators.
    Seventy percent of all superconducting wire.
    What does the cascade say?

[SKIPPY reads. The silence before the number is long.]

SKIPPY:
    Lambda equals zero point two two.
    T_c: below threshold.
    Measured lambda: one point two six.
    Measured T_c: nine point two five Kelvin.
    The cascade is off by a factor of six.

[NIOBIUM does not flinch. It has been misunderstood before.
Somewhere below the stage, THE D-BAND stirs — a low harmonic
the electron gas cannot see.]

THE D-BAND: [from beneath, not yet fully audible]
    I am here.

CAPTAIN:
    The free-electron model cannot see the d-band resonance.
    Niobium has a sharp peak in its density of states
    right at the Fermi level — the d-band.
    That peak amplifies the electron-phonon coupling
    by a factor of five or six.
    Your model has no d-band.
    Your model has a smooth, featureless Fermi sea.

SKIPPY:
    The atom gives the ingredients.
    The lattice gives the emergence.

CAPTAIN:
    We need to treat our matter using a volume of it,
    or we cannot see the transitions and the bonds.
    The volume is where the physics lives.

[Both look at NIOBIUM. NIOBIUM looks back.
It is not an accusation. It is a lesson.]

THE MATTER — NIOBIUM:
    You found aluminum.
    You found rhenium.
    You found the zeros.
    Six elements correct. One element teaches.
    I am not your failure.
    I am your next chapter.

THE D-BAND: [from beneath, clearer now]
    Build the volume.
    Read the density of states.
    Lay the crystal.
    I will be waiting.

[THE ELECTRON GAS sings one last chord — the Fermi sphere
harmonizing with itself, isotropic and free, faithful
to everything it can reach. Beyond its surface, in the
narrowing resonance of d-electrons, THE D-BAND hums
a different frequency entirely — patient, unilluminated,
already present in the crystal that has not yet been built.]

SKIPPY:
    We have caught the snake by the head.
    But the body is longer than we thought.
    The next grip is the volume.

CAPTAIN:
    Then that is where we go.

[The lights contract to a single point above the periodic table.
NIOBIUM stands in it, radiating nine point two five Kelvin,
unexplained and uncomplaining. The chain holds as far as it reaches.
Below the stage, THE D-BAND waits.]

                    — CURTAIN —
