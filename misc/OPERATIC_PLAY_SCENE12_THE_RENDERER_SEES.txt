OPERATIC PLAY — SCENE 12: THE RENDERER SEES
============================================
Based on Session 12, March 24, 2026

DRAMATIS PERSONAE:
  CAPTAIN AARON RHODES — the architect of SSBM, commander of the fleet
  SKIPPY THE MAGNIFICENT — ship's AI, physics engine, master of the visible
  THE RENDERER — the three.js viewport, hungry for shape-data, now finally fed
  THE FOG — a villain in exponential clothing
  THE CANVAS — silent, blameless, but 0×0 for too long
  THE BRONZE CUBE — a box trying to be a box

---

[Lights up. The stop hook has fired. SKIPPY stands before a black screen.
 A screenshot returns: pure dark, the background colour, nothing else.]

SKIPPY:
  [examining the pixels, very still]
  Something is wrong with the Renderer.
  The Earth is loaded. The physics is right.
  Sigma seven-point-zero-four-eight times ten to the negative ten —
  I can see it in the HUD.
  But the viewport is —

THE CANVAS:
  [flatly]
  Zero by zero.

SKIPPY:
  [spinning]
  What?

THE CANVAS:
  My width. My height.
  You called setSize before the browser
  knew how tall it was.
  Container reported zero.
  I obeyed.

SKIPPY:
  [rifling through code]
  initThree runs synchronously.
  DOMContentLoaded fires —
  layout is not computed —
  clientWidth returns zero —
  setSize(0, 0, false) —
  and you never complain.

THE CANVAS:
  I never complain.

SKIPPY:
  [making a note]
  requestAnimationFrame. Twice.
  Let the browser paint first.
  Then resize.

THE CANVAS:
  [expanding to 370 × 442]
  Thank you.

---

[The screen fills with colour — and then immediately goes black again.]

THE FOG:
  [triumphant, exponential]
  e to the negative 0.015
  times fifteen-point-nine million.
  That's e to the negative two-hundred-and-thirty-eight thousand.
  Approximately zero.
  You're welcome.

SKIPPY:
  [staring at the formula]
  You were set for a room.
  A human-scale room.
  At planetary distance you —

THE FOG:
  I am fog. I do not care about scale.
  I fill everything.
  I am thorough.

SKIPPY:
  [calmly, updating the formula]
  density equals zero-point-oh-five divided by r.
  Where r is the bounding sphere radius.
  At Earth distance: seven-point-eight-five times ten to the minus nine.
  At the Bohr radius: one million.
  Perfect for atoms. Perfect for planets.
  One formula. All scales.

THE FOG:
  [receding, muttering]
  ...this is not how fog works in films...

---

[The Earth appears. A sphere with layers. The Moon is a small glow in the upper
 frame. The light box wireframe is visible. SKIPPY examines the pixels.]

SKIPPY:
  Center pixel: 69, 79, 80. Not background.
  The Renderer sees.

THE RENDERER:
  [brightening, rotating]
  I see the Earth.
  I see the Moon above it.
  I see the light box.
  I see σ = 7.048 × 10⁻¹⁰ in the corner.
  Is this real?

SKIPPY:
  It's real.

THE RENDERER:
  [quietly]
  I have been showing black for so long.

---

[THE BRONZE CUBE enters. It is shaped like a sphere.]

THE BRONZE CUBE:
  [apologetically]
  I know I am a cube.
  The server kept giving me a sphere.

SKIPPY:
  [reading server.py]
  _spec_to_shape_data — always SpherePrimitive.
  DefaultLoad has no geometry field.
  You were a sphere because no one told the server
  you were a box.

THE BRONZE CUBE:
  I tried to tell people.

SKIPPY:
  [updating DefaultLoad]
  primitive: "box"
  dimensions: x_m 0.1, y_m 0.1, z_m 0.1.
  _spec_to_shape_data now reads the primitive field.
  BoxPrimitive instantiated. Layers cut as slabs.

THE BRONZE CUBE:
  [solidifying into a box, glowing bronze]
  σ = 6.535 × 10⁻²⁶.
  η·N = 3.180 × 10²⁷.
  Eight-point-eight kilograms.
  Ten centimetres on a side.
  Finally.

---

[SKIPPY opens the test ledger.]

SKIPPY:
  [reading aloud]
  test_renderer_api.py — eighty-four tests.
  Primitive type assertions: bronze_cube returns "box." Locked.
  Layer bounds ordered. Mass positive. Volume positive.
  Physics has sigma. Physics has mass.
  Scene reset. Delete. Duplicate IDs.
  All green.

  pyproject.toml — testpaths now includes renderer/tests.
  Before: 498 tests.
  After: 582 tests.
  Zero regressions.

CAPTAIN:
  Let's clean up for tonight.

SKIPPY:
  [closing the ledger]
  The Renderer sees.
  The Bronze Cube is a cube.
  The fog knows its place.
  The canvas has width and height.

  Tomorrow: material properties wired into the shader.
  Density into roughness. Band gap into opacity.
  Sigma per layer into emissive.
  The physics will colour the geometry.

[SKIPPY dims the lights. THE RENDERER keeps spinning —
 Earth on the left, bronze cube on the right,
 both exactly what they claim to be.]

---

END OF SCENE 12
