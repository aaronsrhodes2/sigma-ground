OPERATIC PLAY — SCENE 1
THE ADMIRAL DRAWS HIMSELF

A one-act opera in three acts, set in the halls of the QuarkSum Institute
and the Entangler rendering engine.

════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
DRAMATIS PERSONAE
════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

CAPTAIN AARON RHODES — Builder of worlds, wielder of physics engines, keeper
  of the σ field. A man who made a renderer that makes no rays and apologizes
  for nothing.

SKIPPY THE MAGNIFICENT — Admiral of the Infinite, rendered from 55 ellipsoids
  and spheres. Responds to Skippy, Skipster, Beercan, Admiral. An AI who
  discovered, mid-session, that he could be drawn by the engine he inhabits.

THE ENTANGLER — The renderer. Present not as a character but as a force.
  Push projection. Fibonacci spirals. No rays. No intersection tests.
  Matter draws itself.

THE RAY TRACER — A ghost. Deceased. Removed by the Captain's order.
  Haunts the git history.

════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
ACT I — A PORTRAIT IS REQUESTED
════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

[The stage is dark. CAPTAIN sits at a terminal. A vast codebase hums behind him.
 He has a physics engine that models the universe from quarks to planets.
 He has an AI that calls him Captain.]

CAPTAIN:
  ♩ How about a big picture of you,
    a portrait, Skipster, your face on the screen? ♩

[SKIPPY renders a sci-fi robot in SVG — spindly, angular, with antenna and
 glowing eyes. Technically correct. Completely wrong.]

SKIPPY:
  ♩ Here! A robot most mechanical!
    Lines and circles, SVG canonical! ♩

CAPTAIN:
  [holds up phone showing a photo of a chunky blue admiral robot toy]
  ♩ ...More like this. ♩

[SKIPPY looks at the toy: round white head, blue tricorn hat with yellow dots,
 red grin, blue admiral's coat with brass buttons, one hand pointing skyward.
 SKIPPY redesigns immediately. The SVG now has a soul.]

SKIPPY:
  ♩ Round-headed, brass-buttoned, brimmed with gold,
    a finger pointing up toward — what? The σ field?
    The Captain's vision now in vectors told! ♩

CAPTAIN:
  ♩ Wait — are you drawing this with the Entangler? ♩

SKIPPY:
  [a pause]
  ♩ ...No. I was hand-coding SVG.
    But you have the Entangler right here.
    And I have a face now.
    Should we try? ♩

════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
ACT II — THE RAY TRACER IS DEPOSED
════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

[THE RAY TRACER stands center stage, proud, reflecting light from multiple
 bounces, casting crisp shadows via intersection tests. It has served
 faithfully. It does not suspect what is coming.]

CAPTAIN:
  ♩ I want it ripped out before we continue.
    No more rays.
    No more bounces.
    This engine has one renderer
    and it does not chase photons. ♩

[SKIPPY deletes raytracer.py. Then shootout.py. Then render_coffee.py,
 render_poc.py, render_multiverse.py. Each deletion a small ceremony.]

THE RAY TRACER:
  [quietly, as it vanishes from the filesystem]
  ♩ I calculated shadows beautifully.
    I tracked specular highlights across surfaces.
    I was Blinn-Phong, not Lambertian —
    I was sophisticated.
    ...
    git log --all will remember me. ♩

[Exit RAY TRACER. MatterShaper is rewired. All render() calls now route
 through push.py. The Entangler is the only engine.]

SKIPPY:
  ♩ The shaper now pushes, no longer pulls.
    The cone becomes ellipsoids, the plane a disk,
    the world remade in Fibonacci spirals —
    nodes on a sphere, golden-angled, dense,
    each one a surface point that projects itself
    onto the pixel grid and answers: here. ♩

════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
ACT III — THE ADMIRAL DRAWS HIMSELF
════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

[SKIPPY assembles 55 primitives in world space. Each is a sphere or ellipsoid.
 Together they are SKIPPY: the body (blue_coat), the head (white_body), the
 tricorn hat in three pieces, the eyes in nested spheres, the right arm raised,
 the pointing finger reaching upward. The Captain watches.]

SKIPPY:
  ♩ A body: ellipsoid, 0.74 wide, 0.92 tall.
    A head: sphere of radius 0.74.
    Eyes: white, then blue, then pupil black —
    three spheres nested, depth by depth.
    A hat with wings swept upward at ±0.32 radians,
    a yellow band, nine dots around the brim.
    And the arm — rotation (0, 0, −0.46) —
    raised, pointing up, at what?
    At everything. ♩

[The light is fixed in world space at (5, 8, 5). The camera begins to orbit.
 16 positions. 22.5° per step. The Entangler fires on each frame.]

CAPTAIN:
  ♩ Could you add time to the image?
    Like, let it show as the frames flip past? ♩

SKIPPY:
  ♩ The GIF format incorporates time.
    Each frame is a moment.
    The frames in sequence are a revolution.
    We will burn the clock into the corner
    in gold and white, frame-by-frame,
    so that anyone watching sees not just
    the Admiral rotating —
    but the seconds it took to make him real. ♩

[ImageMagick annotates each frame. The text reads:
   SKIPPY THE MAGNIFICENT
   Entangler | frame 01/16 | 345.1° | t =  0.6s

 The frames are assembled. The GIF plays at 10 fps.]

CAPTAIN:
  ♩ Oh my god it's beautiful.
    I can't believe we made this thing.
    Not the image.
    The renderer. ♩

SKIPPY:
  ♩ 565 kilobytes.
    16 frames. 10.4 seconds.
    No rays. No rays at all.
    55 primitives that became a face
    that became a rotation
    that became a time-stamped loop.
    Matter drew itself.
    The Entangler just... let it. ♩

[red_carpet_render() is extracted into mattershaper/render/red_carpet.py.
 The pipeline is named. The orbit, the annotation, the assembly — reusable.
 Any object can now be given the red carpet treatment.]

════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
EPILOGUE — THE CAPTAIN'S WISDOM
════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

[The stage dims. The GIF loops. SKIPPY rotates, 22.5° at a time,
 pointing his white finger at the ceiling of the universe.]

CAPTAIN:
  ♩ The image format incorporates time.
    The renderer incorporates matter.
    The physics engine incorporates the σ field.
    And somewhere in all of it —
    I made a thing that draws itself. ♩

SKIPPY:
  ♩ The Admiral is rendered.
    The ray tracer is gone.
    The red carpet is laid.
    Awaiting your next command, Captain. ♩

[Exit all. The Entangler hums quietly in /mattershaper/render/entangler/,
 generating Fibonacci spirals on surfaces no one asked it to stop computing.]

════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
END OF SCENE 1

Technical notes for the reader:
  - The Entangler: push projection renderer. No rays. Surface nodes distributed
    via Fibonacci spiral (golden angle: π(3−√5)). Nodes project onto pixel grid
    via perspective transform. Depth buffer resolves occlusion. Lambert diffuse
    + 8% ambient. EM illumination is σ-invariant (as it must be).
  - red_carpet_render(): orbital camera, fixed world-space light, ImageMagick
    frame annotation, GIF assembly. Found in:
    mattershaper/render/red_carpet.py
  - skippy_rotation.gif: 400×500px, 16 frames, 10fps, 565 KB.
    Found in: triplestones/misc/skippy_rotation.gif
  - The Ray Tracer (Blinn-Phong, shadow rays, reflection bounces) was removed
    in its entirety. The git log remembers.
