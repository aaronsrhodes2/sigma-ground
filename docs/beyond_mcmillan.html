The d-Band Wall

The 13 false negatives (Nb, Ta, Zr, Hf, Y, Os, Ir, Ru, Rh, Lu, Li, Be, Fe) are almost entirely d-band metals where the free-electron pseudopotential cannot capture d-resonance scattering. The electron-phonon coupling in these metals is dominated by the d-band density of states at EF — a volume/lattice property that requires going beyond the Ashcroft empty-core model.

The single false positive (Pd) is the most famous near-miss in superconductivity — paramagnon fluctuations suppress Cooper pairing, an effect not captured by the McMillan formula.
